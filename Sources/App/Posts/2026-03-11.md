### Liminal: Private Skincare Tracking/Routines (Open Beta)
#### March 11, 2026

---

In *On Becoming a Person* (1961), Carl Rogers writes: *"What is most personal is most universal."* That resonated with me and I suspect with many others (ha!). Here's my latest personal thing, which I hope is also universal.

Today I'm announcing the open Beta for **Liminal**, a skincare tracking app I've been working on for the past several months. It started, as most things do, out of a personal need.

I discovered skincare and at some point later, retinoids while trying to deal with acne and learned along the way that they're also among the most effective anti-ageing compounds we have. 

Curiously in certain circles (anti-ageing), using a retinoid is a no-brainer. Since it has long been a dream of mine to help people fight ageing, and the hard truth is that very few skincare ingredients actually do something measurable (retinoids are one of the rare [exceptions](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41302994/)) I felt I could do something to help here.

Now, the tricky part is knowing whether they're working *for you*. Most retinoids take many months to show results, and without a structured way to track your skin over time, it's not easy to separate signal from noise. I wanted something focused, simple, privacy-friendly (I don't feel comfortable uploading my skin to some random server. Liminal stores your sensitive data locally), and effective and nothing out there felt quite right.


---

**Meet Liminal**

Liminal is a skincare tracking app for iOS built around three simple ideas: your **products**, your **routines**, and your **skin check-ins**.

Every day, or every other day, whatever your schedule, you do a quick check-in: snap a photo, rate how your skin is feeling across dimensions like irritation, dryness, and acne, and log which products you applied. Over time, those small data points build a real picture of how your skin is responding to what you're putting on it.

**Insights** tab shows a monthly calendar of your check-in history, a progress chart of your skin scores over time, and a streak badge to keep you consistent.

**Products** tab lets you catalog your shelf with categories and expiration dates.

**Routines** groups products into routines and allows you to schedule a notification.

While I'm using it to track retinoid usage (Adapalene), you can track any other product you're using such as Azelaic Acid or anything else you want to understand if it does good for your skin or not.
